Anatomy and Biology

Body length ranges from 18 to 22.5 inches (45 to 57 centimeters), with the tail adding on an extra 11 to 14 inches (28 to 35 centimeters). Adult sand cats weigh between 3 to 7.5 pounds (1 to 3.5 kilograms).
In human care, sand cats can live to be 13 years old. Lifespan in the wild is unknown.
Unique physical characteristics = Sand cats have a broad head with large eyes and low-set ears. They have short limbs. The tail, which can account for about half of the head-body length, features two or three rings and a black tip.
